1. Business Problem & Project Context

In digital optimization and growth engineering, relying on pure intuition to modify user interfaces often leads to friction and lost revenue. This analysis evaluates an e-commerce platform's experimental redesign, pitting the legacy product layout (Design A) against a newly optimized alternative (Design B).

The objective is to establish statistical verification regarding whether Design B drives meaningful increases in customer engagement and absolute purchase transactions before executing a full-scale deployment.

2. Experimental Scope & Evaluation Methodology

The experiment segregates incoming traffic into control and treatment groups to measure two critical performance tracking indicators:

  • User Session Duration (Continuous Metric): Quantifies active engagement and surface attention. Analyzed using a Two-Sample Independent T-Test to evaluate differences between continuous means.
  • User Purchase Action (Categorical Metric): Quantifies actual customer conversions. Analyzed using a Chi-Square ($\chi^2$) Test of Independence to confirm shifts in distribution rates.

All statistical thresholds are maintained against a strict significance standard of $\alpha = 0.05$ (95% Confidence Interval).

4. Actionable Strategic Recommendations
  • Full Production Rollout: Given that both continuous interaction velocity and absolute bottom-line conversion values have demonstrated high statistical significance, it is recommended to fully deploy Design B to replace Design A across 100% of live application traffic.
  • Capital Efficiency & ROI Estimation: The observed transition from a 30% baseline to a 50% conversion represents a 66.7% relative surge in transactions. This directly increases e-commerce revenue and optimizes profit thresholds without expanding digital marketing spend or increasing Customer Acquisition Costs (CAC).
